# Expert Councils and Specialist Operating Model

Proof of Life Chain needs more than engineers. It needs a professional constitutional, social, medical, education, economic, and governance operating model. The DAO should create domain councils whose recommendations become auditable protocol improvement proposals.

## Council principles

- Councils advise and draft standards; they do not secretly control user rights.
- Every sensitive decision needs public rationale, review windows, and appeal mechanisms.
- Members must disclose conflicts of interest.
- Council seats should rotate and include citizen oversight.
- Protocol rules must be translated into software only after legal, ethical, and security review.

## Required specialist groups

### Constitutional and legislative council

Suggested seats:

- constitutional lawyer;
- parliamentary or congressional drafting expert;
- former regulator or senior civil servant;
- human-rights and civil-liberties advocate;
- public procurement specialist;
- international law advisor.

Mandate:

- draft model legislation and regulatory sandboxes;
- define lawful government use of identity, welfare, voting, and citizenship modules;
- ensure compatibility with constitutions and treaties;
- require appeal paths and judicial review for adverse decisions.

### Health and proof-of-health council

Suggested seats:

- physician or public-health expert;
- healthcare privacy officer;
- biometric security specialist;
- wearable-device engineer;
- patient advocate;
- health economist.

Mandate:

- define proof-of-health attestations without exposing medical records;
- approve clinical and wearable-data schemas;
- prevent insurance, employment, or political misuse;
- design consent and revocation policies.

### Education and future skills council

Suggested seats:

- education reform scholar;
- vocational training leader;
- university credentialing officer;
- youth representative;
- employer/apprenticeship representative;
- verifiable credential engineer.

Mandate:

- modernize education credentials beyond legacy schooling;
- support lifelong learning and skills passports;
- prevent credential fraud;
- make records portable and citizen-owned.

### Welfare and social protection council

Suggested seats:

- welfare policy expert;
- social worker;
- fraud analytics expert;
- beneficiary advocate;
- municipal service representative;
- data protection officer.

Mandate:

- reduce duplicate claims and organized fraud;
- protect legitimate beneficiaries from wrongful denial;
- require explainable eligibility checks;
- create human appeal and hardship pathways.

### Security, voting, and civic trust council

Suggested seats:

- election integrity expert;
- cybersecurity engineer;
- law-enforcement oversight representative;
- civil liberties advocate;
- disaster response official;
- DAO governance researcher.

Mandate:

- define privacy-preserving voting eligibility;
- prevent coercion and surveillance;
- authorize sensitive access-control attestations;
- create emergency-use limits.

### Economics, incentives, and treasury council

Suggested seats:

- token economist;
- public finance expert;
- validator operations lead;
- anti-corruption auditor;
- impact investor or grant expert;
- community treasury representative.

Mandate:

- define validator rewards and slashing;
- fund audits and public goods;
- model costs for governments and citizens;
- prevent token capture and pay-to-rule governance.

## Proposal workflow

1. Domain council drafts a standard.
2. Security and privacy reviewers produce risk notes.
3. Legal council checks jurisdiction and rights impact.
4. Engineers create a protocol improvement proposal.
5. DAO opens a public comment window.
6. Governance vote approves, rejects, or sends back for revision.
7. Timelock executes upgrade after audit sign-off.
