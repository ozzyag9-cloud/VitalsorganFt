# Mauritius Pilot Case Study Blueprint

## Why Mauritius

Mauritius is small enough to pilot national digital public infrastructure responsibly and sophisticated enough to become a global reference implementation. A successful pilot can show how privacy-preserving identity, education, health, welfare, and civic participation can work without building a surveillance state.

## Pilot narrative

Proof of Life Chain can be presented as a public-good trust infrastructure, not merely a crypto product. The pitch should emphasize:

- citizen-owned credentials;
- fraud-resistant public services;
- lower administrative overhead;
- transparent and appealable welfare validation;
- modern education and skills records;
- privacy-preserving health and liveness attestations;
- open-source auditability;
- government, university, hospital, NGO, and citizen participation.

## Proposed phases

### Phase 1: Trust and credentials sandbox

- Launch voluntary education and professional credential attestations.
- Onboard universities, training providers, and professional bodies.
- Publish open schemas and privacy assessments.

### Phase 2: Proof-of-life and health attestations

- Add opt-in liveness attestations for services where in-person checks are costly.
- Integrate healthcare providers only through encrypted references and proof hashes.
- Commission medical privacy review before production use.

### Phase 3: Welfare integrity pilot

- Select a narrow benefit program with clear eligibility rules.
- Use duplicate-claim prevention and proof-of-eligibility commitments.
- Maintain human caseworker review and appeal rights.
- Publish metrics on fraud reduction and wrongful-denial prevention.

### Phase 4: Civic DAO consultation

- Deploy a non-binding citizen consultation DAO.
- Use proof-of-personhood and residency eligibility without publishing votes linked to identity.
- Report participation, policy sentiment, and governance lessons.

## Government proposal package

- Executive briefing.
- Legal and constitutional memo.
- Privacy impact assessment.
- Technical architecture.
- Security and audit plan.
- Procurement and implementation plan.
- Citizen communications plan.
- Cost-benefit model.

## Success metrics

- reduced duplicate claims;
- reduced manual verification time;
- credential verification time below one minute;
- zero raw biometric or medical records on-chain;
- appeal response times tracked and published;
- public audit reports completed;
- citizen trust measured through independent surveys.
