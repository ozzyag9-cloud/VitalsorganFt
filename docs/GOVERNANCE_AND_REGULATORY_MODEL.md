# Governance and Regulatory Model

## Design goal

Proof of Life Chain should combine decentralized execution with lawful public oversight. The protocol can be globally open like Bitcoin, while its government-facing modules remain jurisdiction-aware, rights-preserving, and auditable.

## Checks and balances

- **DAO:** votes on protocol parameters, treasury, public-goods funding, and upgrades.
- **Expert councils:** draft standards and risk assessments for each domain.
- **Public institutions:** operate validators or issuers under public rules.
- **Independent auditors:** review contracts, circuits, security, and privacy impact.
- **Ombuds and appeal bodies:** protect people from wrongful exclusion or automated harm.
- **Courts and regulators:** remain the authority for legal rights, citizenship, and due process.

## Executive oversight without central capture

Executive or government participation should be structured as transparent roles, not unilateral control. Recommended controls:

- emergency pause only for narrow domains and limited durations;
- public incident report requirement;
- multi-signature approval including independent members;
- post-action DAO and legal review;
- no emergency power to reveal private data or seize identity credentials.

## Legislative blueprint modules

1. Recognition of privacy-preserving digital attestations.
2. Rules for trusted issuers and validator licensing.
3. Data minimization and prohibition on raw sensitive data on-chain.
4. Appeal rights for welfare, health, voting, and citizenship decisions.
5. Audit requirements for smart contracts and ZK circuits.
6. Public procurement and open-source requirements.
7. Anti-corruption and conflict-of-interest disclosures.

## Non-negotiable safeguards

- No raw biometric, medical, voting, or welfare case data on public ledgers.
- No automated denial of essential services without human review.
- No citizenship or migration module without local constitutional review.
- No hidden scoring system for people.
- No black-box validator decisions in public service contexts.
